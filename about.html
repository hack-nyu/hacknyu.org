About

HackNYU is a 48-hour hackathon taking place this March 23–25th, hosted at NYU locations around the world. Come to build a project, learn with our talks and mentors, and win prizes. This year, we have four hack categories for you to build for: Accessibility & Assistive Technology, Education Technology, Healthcare, and Sustainability & Social Impact. HackNYU is free, and made possible thanks to our wonderful sponsors and volunteers.

FAQ

Who can participate?

For our Brooklyn event, anyone in high school, or college (even graduate students) can attend — no need to be an NYU student! If you're under 18, you will be required to bring a liability waiver signed by your parent or guardian. This form will be linked on the registration page.

Attendees in Shanghai and Abu Dabi must be enrolled at NYU.

Where is HackNYU?

HackNYU is primarily taking place at NYU Tandon School of Engineering, 6 MetroTech Center, Brooklyn, NY 11201, which is easily accessible via the subway (right by Jay Street–MetroTech). Students enrolled in NYU Shanghai and Abu Dabi can attend their local events on campus.

Will there be travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, we are unable to provide travel reimbursements or send buses to nearby cities this year. We apologize for the inconvenience, and hope you can still make it!

How will projects be judged?

HackNYU has 4 possible tracks (or categories) that you can build a project towards. These are:

  • Sustainability & Social Impact
  • Healthcare
  • Accessbility & Assitive Technology
  • Education Technology

Each category will have its own judges and prizes. Each track will have 3 winning teams. Learn more about the tracks here.
